How Tier Matching Works

For High-Volume Baccarat Players
What Is a Tier Match?

If you are a rated high-roller at a major casino — holding Diamond, Chairman, Noir, Seven Stars, Prestige, or equivalent elite status — you can often request matching status at a competing property before you arrive or during your first visit. Casinos compete fiercely for high-volume baccarat players, and a documented play history is your leverage.

How to Request One
  • 1
    Contact the casino's VIP Host department or International Marketing directly — not the front desk or general reservations line. Ask specifically for the host who handles table game players or baccarat.
  • 2
    Provide proof of your current status: a photo of your card, a recent comp statement, or a letter from your home casino host confirming your average daily theoretical (ADT) and visit frequency.
  • 3
    Request a complimentary trial visit or a status match at or near your existing tier. Many Las Vegas, Macau, and Singapore properties will extend a matching rate or temporary elite status in exchange for a play commitment.
  • 4
    Negotiate the comp package: room, food and beverage, transportation, and airfare credit are all on the table for high-volume players. Get the terms in writing from the host before arriving.
  • 5
    Play to the agreed level and ask for a written reference from the host at checkout — this becomes your credential for the next tier match at a third property.
Which Properties Tier-Match Most Aggressively

Las Vegas Strip properties (especially MGM and Caesars portfolios) frequently match status across their own networks and will negotiate with competitors for new high-value players. Macau VIP rooms rely almost entirely on host relationships and junket operators rather than loyalty card status. Singapore's MBS and RWS both have dedicated international VIP teams. In Macau, ask for a premium mass or direct VIP contact rather than a junket.

Important: Tier matching is not a published policy at most casinos — it is a negotiation. The higher your documented average bet and annual play, the stronger your position. Players averaging $500+ per hand with significant volume (100+ hours/year) are typically well-positioned to request matching. First-time requests are usually for a trial visit, not a permanent status grant. Always confirm with the host what play is expected to maintain any status extended to you.
