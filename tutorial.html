Chapter 1

What is Baccarat?

Baccarat is one of the simplest casino card games in existence. Two hands are dealt — Banker and Player — and you bet on which one will have a score closer to 9. That's the entire premise. No skill decides who wins; the cards fall by fixed rules every single time.

Why Baccarat?
Baccarat has one of the lowest house edges in the casino. Betting Banker carries only a ~1.06% house edge. Even Player bet sits at ~1.24%. Compare this to American roulette at 5.26% — baccarat is far friendlier to the bettor.

The Modulo Rule — Only the Last Digit Counts
Scores always drop to a single digit. If two cards sum to 10 or more, drop the tens digit:

K + 7 = 0 + 7 = 7  |  6 + 7 = 13 → 3  |  9 + 9 = 18 → 8

You can never "bust" in baccarat. The worst hand is 0 (called "Baccarat"), and the best is 9.

Natural 9 — "La Grande"
The highest possible natural. If one side has a natural 9, it can only lose to another natural 9 (a tie). Nothing beats it.
Natural 8 — "La Petite"
Second-best natural. Natural 8 beats everything except Natural 9. If both hands have 8, it's a Tie.
Natural Tie
If both hands get the same natural (e.g., both 9s), the hand is a Tie. Tie bets pay 8:1. Banker and Player bets are returned (pushed).
Naturals happen ~34% of the time
About 1 in 3 hands produces a natural (8 or 9 in 2 cards). When the live feed shows gold text saying "A Natural!" — that's this moment. The roadmap also marks naturals with a yellow dot on the Big Road.

Do the Roads Predict the Future?
No. Every baccarat hand is statistically independent — the shoe has no memory, and past results cannot predict future ones. The roads are a cultural tradition, a visualization tool, and a way to notice streaks and chop patterns that have already happened. Experienced players use them to stay engaged with the shoe's "feel," not as a mathematical edge.

Chapter 12

Strategy Tips for New Players

Baccarat is a game of chance — no strategy guarantees wins. But intelligent habits separate players who enjoy the game from players who burn through their balance fast. Here's what to know.

01
Always Bet Banker as Your Default
Banker wins ~50.7% of decisive hands. Even after the 5% commission in Standard mode, it remains the lowest house-edge bet. Make Banker your baseline until you have a specific reason to switch.
02
Avoid the Tie Bet
Tie pays 8:1 but carries a ~14.4% house edge. It hits only ~9.5% of the time. The allure is real — but mathematically, every dollar on Tie costs far more than Banker or Player.
03
Set a Stop-Loss Before You Start
Decide how much you're willing to lose before opening the game — then stick to it. When you hit your limit, close the session. Like Steven the bot, a hard stop-loss is your best risk tool.
04
Use EZ Mode for Practice
No commission math, familiar rules, and the Dragon 7 / Panda 8 side bets add excitement without complexity. It's the best mode for learning all the mechanics cleanly.
05
Side Bets Are Entertainment, Not Strategy
Place Dragon 7 or Tiger bets sparingly and for fun. The house edges are 3–10× higher than main bets. Small side bet, big main bet — not the other way around.
06
Don't Chase Losses
Doubling down after a string of losses (the Martingale) requires exponential bankroll and can hit table limits fast. Flat betting or modest progressions are far safer over a session.
07
Use the Repeat Bet Button
When you've found a betting combination you're comfortable with, the Repeat Bet button restores it instantly each hand — saving time and preventing accidental mis-clicks during exciting streaks.
08
Read the Board — Don't Chase It
If the Big Road shows 8 straight Banker wins, betting Banker "because it's on a streak" has the exact same odds as any other hand. The roads reveal history, not fate. Enjoy the pattern without over-betting it.
