Main Bets — Standard 8-Deck Game

Baccarat has three main bets. Two of them (Banker and Player) are among the best bets in any casino. Tie is one of the worst bets in any casino. The mathematics are fixed — no strategy or system can change them.

Bet Payout Win Probability House Edge Verdict
Banker 0.95:1 (after 5% commission) 45.86% 1.06% Best Bet
Player 1:1 44.62% 1.24% Very Good
Tie 8:1 (some tables 9:1) 9.51% 14.36% (4.84% at 9:1) Avoid
Why Banker Wins More
Banker wins more often (45.86% vs 44.62%) because of the third-card drawing rules, which were designed to give the Banker hand a mathematical advantage. The casino captures this by charging a 5% commission on Banker wins. Even after the commission, Banker remains the better bet by 0.18 percentage points.

The Super 6 Trap
Super 6 markets itself as "commission-free baccarat" but it's actually the worst commission variant for the player. The Banker-wins-on-6 penalty costs more than the eliminated commission saves. Always prefer EZ Baccarat over Super 6 if given the choice between commission-free options.

The Rule on Side Bets
No baccarat side bet has a house edge below 2%. The lowest documented baccarat side bet edge is Dragon Bonus Player at ~2.65%. The main Banker bet at 1.06% is mathematically superior to every side bet ever offered on a baccarat table. Play side bets for entertainment, knowing that is what you are paying for.

Streaks Are Normal — They Prove Nothing
A 6-hand winning streak for Banker happens roughly once every 100 hands on average. Players see this as a "hot shoe" — the mathematics sees it as an unremarkable event in a large sample. The probability of the next hand being Banker is always 45.86%, regardless of what the previous 20 hands were.
Hold % vs. House Edge — Know the Difference

Casinos report "hold percentage" to investors and regulators. Players often confuse hold% with house edge. They are completely different numbers that measure different things.

MetricDefinitionTypical Baccarat ValueWho It Affects
House Edge % of each bet the casino mathematically retains 1.06% (Banker) Individual player per bet
Hold % Casino win ÷ total chips bought (drop) 15–25% average Casino profitability per table
Why Hold % Is So Much Higher Than House Edge
A player who buys $10,000 in chips and plays for several hours bets the same chips many times over. If they bet 10 rounds averaging $1,000, they've wagered $10,000 on a $10,000 buy-in — but by round 20 they've effectively "churned" $20,000 through the 1.06% edge. The casino's hold is the cumulative effect of that churn, not the per-bet edge. High-volatility whale play makes hold% swing wildly: one big win or loss can move a month's baccarat hold by 10+ percentage points.

The One Thing Baccarat Strategy Cannot Do
No betting system, pattern observation, shoe analysis, card counting method, or superstition changes the house edge. The edge is fixed by the rules. What strategy can do: minimize it (bet Banker, avoid Tie), manage your bankroll to stay in action longer, and ensure that your experience is the one you chose — not the one variance chose for you.
