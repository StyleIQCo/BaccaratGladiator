3. Third-Card Rules

These rules are fixed — neither you nor the dealer makes a choice. The third card is drawn automatically based on the two-card totals. You never need to memorize these to play, but understanding them helps you read why a hand unfolded the way it did.

Player Third-Card Rule

Player Total (2 cards)Action
0, 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5Draw a third card
6 or 7Stand — no third card
8 or 9Natural — hand ends immediately

Banker Third-Card Rule

The Banker rule depends on whether the Player drew a third card and what that card was.

If Player did NOT draw a third card (Player stood on 6 or 7):

Banker TotalAction
0 – 5Draw
6 – 7Stand
8 – 9Natural — hand ends

If Player DID draw a third card:

Banker TotalDraws when Player's 3rd card is…Stands when…
0, 1, or 2Always draws—
30, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 98
42, 3, 4, 5, 6, 70, 1, 8, 9
54, 5, 6, 70, 1, 2, 3, 8, 9
66, 70, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 8, 9
7Always stands—
8 – 9Natural — hand ends—
Memory shortcut: Banker on 3 draws unless Player's third card was an 8. Banker on 6 only draws if Player's third card was a 6 or 7. Everything else follows the pattern of drawing on low totals and standing on high.
Practice tip: Baccarat Gladiator applies all third-card rules automatically. Watch the cards deal and you'll internalize the rules naturally within a few shoes.
