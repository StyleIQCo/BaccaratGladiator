6. Strategy & Casino Etiquette

The Only True Baccarat Strategy

Baccarat is a fixed-odds game — no decision you make changes the outcome of the cards. The closest thing to an optimal strategy is a short list of smart habits:

SituationBest ActionWhy
Which side to bet?Banker1.06% edge vs 1.24% for Player
Tie bet is temptingSkip it14.4% house edge — 11× worse than Banker
On a Player streakStay with PlayerPattern-following is fine; the edge doesn't change
Should I card count?Not worth itEV gain is less than 0.01% per hand
Down 40% on the sessionStop and leaveChasing losses increases risk without changing the odds

Casino Etiquette — Do

  • Place bets before the dealer calls "no more bets"
  • Keep chips neatly stacked in your betting zone
  • Handle squeeze cards with one hand
  • Tip the dealer on a natural or a big win
  • Know the table minimum before sitting down

Casino Etiquette — Don't

  • Bend, crease, or violently damage squeeze cards
  • Touch your bet after the dealer signals "no more bets"
  • Give unsolicited advice to other players
  • Blame the dealer — they don't control the cards
  • Chase losses beyond your planned stop-loss

The Squeeze

In high-limit rooms, players are allowed to squeeze the cards — slowly peeling back the edges to build suspense before the full reveal. This is purely theatrical and doesn't affect the outcome, but it is one of baccarat's most electrifying rituals and the moment most shared on social media.
