5. Betting Systems

No betting system can change the house edge — but they can change how you experience a session. A flat-bet grinder feels different from a Martingale player even when both lose the same expected amount over time. Choose the system that matches your risk tolerance and session goals.

Important: Every hand in baccarat is statistically independent. No system predicts or guarantees wins. Use systems to manage your bankroll behaviour, not to beat the house.
Flat Bet [Low Risk]
Bet the same amount every hand.

Best for: long sessions, lowest variance, pure enjoyment. Slowest growth during winning streaks.

Paroli [Low Risk]
Win: double up (max 3 wins), then reset to 1 unit.

Best for: riding hot streaks without risking original bankroll. Requires 3 consecutive wins to max out the cycle.

1-3-2-6 [Medium Risk]
Bet sequence: 1 → 3 → 2 → 6, then reset. Reset on any loss.

Best for: balanced play — limits downside while capturing 4-win runs. Losing on the 3rd or 4th bet costs the cycle profit.

Fibonacci [Medium Risk]
Sequence: 1 · 1 · 2 · 3 · 5 · 8 · 13 · 21… Step back 2 on a win.

Best for: slower negative progression than Martingale. Long losing runs still escalate quickly.

Martingale [High Risk]
Double your bet after every loss. Reset to 1 unit on a win.

Best for: short sessions near table minimum. Warning: 6 straight losses turns 1 unit into 64 units — table limits and bankroll will end the run before you recover.
